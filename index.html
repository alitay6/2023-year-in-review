Unsustainable Global Lifestyle

As of 2022, the ecological footprint of the world was about 1.75. That means that at our current lifestyle, we'd need nearly 2 Earths to support it.

A great way to counteract these trends is to switch to green energy like solar, wind, and hydro power. By switching to such renewable energy sources, we won’t run the risk of exhausting the natural resources available on Earth, or damaging the environment in the process. Switching to green energy not only makes the environment healthy, but humanity as well.

Earth Overshoot Day

Every year, we reach a day when our demand for resources exceeds what the Earth is biologically able to generate. This is called Earth Overshoot Day. This is calculated by the Global Footprint Network. According to them, we use over 75% of what the Earth’s ecosystems can regenerate. In 2023, Earth Overshoot Day fell on August 2nd. This day basically signifies the day in which the world has used up all the available resources for that entire year. Since the 70s, when the first data was available, Overshoot Day has been coming earlier and earlier.

The Top 5 Green Energy Projects of 2023

List compiled by NES Fircroft, a global energy industry staffing provider.

1. Dogger Bank Wind Farm

Located North of England, the Dogger Bank Wind Farm is being built in 3 phases and will become the world’s largest offshore wind farm upon its project completion in 2026. One single rotation of a wind turbine from this farm can generate enough power to completely power a British household for 2 days. Each turbine will be able to generate enough power to run 16,000 British households, saving about 9,000 vehicles worth of emissions. This wind farm is estimated to last 35 years!

2. Iberdrola Solar Energy Plant

Approved in 2023, this solar energy plant will be built in Portugal and will become the 5th largest in Europe, 5th globally. Expected to be completed in 2025, the plant will generate enough electricity to power 430,000 Portuguese households. With this project in motion, the need for fossil fuels decreases, and Portugal’s annual gas consumption may be reduced by 370 million cubic meters. Portugal has a goal of reaching carbon neutrality by 2050, and this project will aid in reaching that.

3. Tyrsky Wind Farm

Located off the coast of Finland, the Swedish company OX2 began initial developments of the farm this year. The farm is expected to be completed by 2030. The farm will have about 100 wind turbines. Tyrsky will be able to generate 6 TWh (terawatt hours) a year; a terawatt is one trillion watts. The company OX2 currently has 2 other offshore wind farms in Finland, and is planning more. Finland has a goal of reaching carbon neutrality by 2035.

4. Yellow Pine Solar

Yellow Pine Solar company is under NextEra Energy Resources, the largest wind and solar energy producing company in the world. The solar power project will be built in Nevada, US and will cover about 3,000 acres of land. While this project is still in development as of this year, it’s expected to begin being built in 2024, and completed in 2025. It is expected that 500,000 metric tons of carbon dioxide emissions will be offset every year, and will be capable of producing enough clean energy to power 100,000 American homes.

Delta II Wind Project

Under the company Repsol, the Delta II wind project will be an onshore wind farm in northeast Spain. The Delta II Project is scheduled for completion in 2023, and will consist of 26 wind farms. It is expected to be able to power around 800,000 Spanish households and be able to reduce 2.6 million tons of carbon dioxide emissions. The Repsol company aims to reach carbon neutrality by 2050.

Summary

While not all of these projects were put into commission in 2023, the planning, approving, building, and all stages involved are great steps towards transitioning to green energy across the globe. Not only are these projects investing in the future of lowering carbon emissions, but will create many jobs and boost the economy.
